# Part 1: For Patients and Caregivers

<a name="what-is-nc-ask-patients"></a>
## What is NC-ASK?

NC-ASK (North Carolina Autism Support Knowledge) is a free, confidential online tool that helps you find reliable information about autism support resources in North Carolina.

**What NC-ASK can help with:**
- Finding local autism support services
- Learning about therapy options
- Understanding educational resources
- Connecting with community programs
- Getting information about diagnostic services
- Finding financial assistance programs

**What NC-ASK is NOT:**
- Not a replacement for professional medical advice
- Not a diagnostic tool
- Not an emergency service
- Not a therapy or treatment provider

**If you are experiencing a crisis**, please see the [Crisis Support](#crisis-support-patients) section immediately or call 988.

<a name="asking-questions-patients"></a>
## Asking Questions

### How to Ask a Question

1. Type your question in the text box
2. Click the "Ask" button or press Enter
3. Wait a few seconds for NC-ASK to search and prepare your answer
4. Read the response and click on citations to learn more

[Image: Question input box with example question typed in]

### Good Question Examples

**Specific questions work best:**
- "What autism therapy services are available in Raleigh?"
- "How do I apply for Medicaid waiver programs in North Carolina?"
- "Where can I find social skills groups for teenagers with autism in Charlotte?"
- "What early intervention services does NC offer for toddlers?"

**Avoid vague questions:**
- "Tell me about autism"
- "Help me"
- "What should I do?"

### Tips for Better Questions

**Be specific about:**
- **Location**: Include your city or county when asking about local services
- **Age**: Mention the age of the person (child, teen, adult)
- **Topic**: Specify what type of help you need (therapy, education, financial, etc.)

**Example:**
Instead of: "Are there programs for autism?"
Try: "What after-school programs are available for elementary-age children with autism in Durham County?"

<a name="understanding-responses-patients"></a>
## Understanding Responses

### How Responses Are Structured

Every NC-ASK response includes:

1. **Main Answer**: Direct response to your question in plain language
2. **Citations**: Sources where the information came from
3. **Related Information**: Additional context or related resources

[Image: Annotated screenshot showing these three parts of a response]

### Understanding Citations

Citations show you where NC-ASK found the information. They look like this:

**[1] NC Division of Health and Human Services - Autism Services**

You can click on any citation number to see:
- The source organization or document
- When the information was published
- A direct link to the original source

**Why citations matter:**
- Verify the information is from trusted sources
- Get more detailed information from the original document
- Ensure information is current

[Image: Example citation with annotation pointing to source name, date, and link]

### What the Response Includes

**Organizations and Programs**: Names, contact information, and how to apply

**Eligibility Requirements**: Who can use the service and any requirements

**How to Access**: Steps to sign up, apply, or get started

**Important Details**: Costs, wait times, locations, or other key facts

<a name="privacy-patients"></a>
## Privacy and Your Information

### What We Collect

**NC-ASK does NOT collect:**
- Your name
- Your email address
- Your location (beyond what you choose to share in your question)
- Any personal health information
- Any identifying information about you

**NC-ASK does log:**
- The questions asked (but not who asked them)
- Whether crisis keywords were detected
- General usage statistics

### Your Privacy is Protected

- **No account required**: You don't need to create an account or log in
- **Anonymous**: Your questions are not linked to you personally
- **No tracking**: We don't track your browsing or create profiles
- **Secure**: All communication is encrypted

### Safe to Use

You can safely ask questions about:
- Autism resources in your area
- Programs and services
- General information about support options

**Do not include:**
- Personal medical information
- Names of individuals
- Specific addresses or phone numbers
- Financial account information

<a name="crisis-support-patients"></a>
## Crisis Support and Safety

### When to Seek Immediate Help

**Call 911 if:**
- Someone is in immediate danger
- Someone is threatening to hurt themselves or others
- A medical emergency is occurring

**Call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) if:**
- You or someone you know is having thoughts of suicide
- You are experiencing a mental health crisis
- You need emotional support during a crisis

### Crisis Resources

**988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline**
- Available 24/7
- Call or text 988
- Free, confidential support
- Website: 988lifeline.org

**Crisis Text Line**
- Text "HELLO" to 741741
- 24/7 text-based crisis support
- Trained crisis counselors

**NC Hope4NC Helpline**
- Call 1-855-587-3463
- North Carolina-specific mental health support
- Available 24/7

**Autism-Specific Crisis Resources**
- [Will be populated with NC-specific autism crisis resources]

[Image: Crisis resources card with phone numbers prominently displayed]

### NC-ASK Crisis Detection

If NC-ASK detects that your question indicates a possible crisis, you will see a **prominent alert** with crisis resources displayed immediately.

This is designed to help you get the right support quickly. NC-ASK cannot replace emergency services or crisis counseling.

<a name="tips-patients"></a>
## Tips for Better Results

### Make Your Questions Specific

**Instead of:** "Services for autism"
**Try:** "What occupational therapy services accept Medicaid for children with autism in Wake County?"

### Include Relevant Details

- Age group (toddler, child, teen, adult)
- Location (city, county, or region)
- Specific needs (therapy, education, social, financial)

### One Question at a Time

Ask one clear question rather than multiple questions in one message.

**Instead of:** "Where are therapies and schools and how do I pay and what about adults?"
**Try asking separately:**
1. "What therapy options are available for children with autism in Greensboro?"
2. "How can I find autism-friendly schools in Guilford County?"
3. "What funding options help pay for autism therapy in NC?"

### Follow-Up Questions

You can ask follow-up questions to get more specific information:

**First question:** "What is the NC Innovations Waiver?"
**Follow-up:** "How do I apply for the NC Innovations Waiver in Mecklenburg County?"

### Verify Important Information

- Always click on citations to verify information
- Check that sources are current (look at publication dates)
- Contact organizations directly for the most up-to-date details
- Confirm eligibility and requirements before applying

<a name="using-provider-view"></a>
## Using the Provider View

### Asking Clinical Queries

**Structure your queries with clinical specificity:**

**Include:**
- Age and developmental level
- Geographic location (county or city)
- Insurance/funding status
- Specific service types
- Co-occurring conditions if relevant

**Effective query example:**
> "What autism diagnostic services in Buncombe County accept Medicaid and can evaluate a 4-year-old with suspected ASD and language delay?"

**Less effective:**
> "Autism testing in Asheville"

### Query Best Practices

**1. Be Specific About Service Type**
- Specify ABA, OT, SLP, diagnostic, behavioral support, etc.
- Include setting preferences (clinic-based, home-based, school-based)

**2. Include Relevant Clinical Details**
- Age and developmental stage
- Severity level or support needs
- Co-occurring conditions (if relevant to service need)

**3. Specify Practical Constraints**
- Insurance requirements
- Geographic limitations
- Language preferences
- Accessibility needs

### Interpreting Query Results

[Image: Annotated Provider View response showing key elements]

**Response Components:**

**1. Direct Answer**
- Synthesized from authoritative sources
- Plain language summary
- Key facts highlighted

**2. Citations and Sources**
- Numbered references
- Source organization/document
- Publication or last-update date
- Direct links to source material

**3. Evidence Quality**
- Government sources (highest confidence)
- Institutional resources (high confidence)
- Community organizations (verify independently)

### Verifying Information

**Best Practices:**
1. **Check citation dates**: Ensure information is current
2. **Review source documents**: Click citations for complete context
3. **Verify with programs directly**: Contact organizations to confirm:
   - Current availability
   - Waitlist status
   - Insurance acceptance
   - Eligibility criteria

4. **Cross-reference critical information**: For treatment decisions or major resource recommendations, verify through multiple sources

<a name="interpreting-responses-providers"></a>
## Interpreting Responses and Citations

### Citation Format

Citations appear as numbered references: **[1]**, **[2]**, etc.

**Click any citation number** to view:
- Full source name and organization
- Document title
- Publication or update date
- Direct URL to source material

[Image: Citation detail popup showing all fields]

### Source Reliability Hierarchy

**Tier 1: Official Government Sources**
- NC DHHS (Department of Health and Human Services)
- NC DPI (Department of Public Instruction)
- Federal agencies (CDC, HRSA, CMS)
- State Medicaid documentation

**Tier 2: Medical and Academic Institutions**
- University medical centers
- Children's hospitals
- Academic research publications

**Tier 3: Established Organizations**
- Autism Society of NC
- The Arc of NC
- National autism organizations (ASAN, Autism Speaks)

**Tier 4: Community Organizations**
- Local support groups
- Community programs
- Private providers

**Clinical Recommendation:**
For critical clinical decisions or resource recommendations, prioritize Tier 1 and Tier 2 sources and verify information directly with organizations.

### Understanding Response Limitations

**NC-ASK responses are limited by:**

1. **Knowledge Base Scope**
   - Only includes documents ingested into the system
   - May not include very recent programs or changes
   - Geographic coverage may vary

2. **Information Currency**
   - Sources are current as of last knowledge base update
   - Programs may have changed availability, eligibility, or contact info
   - Always verify time-sensitive information

3. **AI Synthesis**
   - Responses are generated by AI based on source documents
   - May not capture nuanced clinical scenarios
   - Cannot provide personalized medical advice

**Clinical Judgment Required:**
NC-ASK provides information, not recommendations. Clinical decision-making should integrate:
- Patient-specific factors
- Clinical assessment findings
- Family preferences and priorities
- Your professional judgment and expertise

<a name="limitations-providers"></a>
## Limitations and Disclaimers

### System Limitations

**NC-ASK is NOT:**

**A diagnostic tool**
- Cannot diagnose autism spectrum disorder
- Cannot assess developmental delays
- Cannot interpret diagnostic test results

**A treatment decision tool**
- Cannot recommend specific treatments for individual patients
- Cannot compare treatment efficacy for specific cases
- Cannot provide medication guidance

**Real-time or comprehensive**
- Knowledge base updated periodically (not continuously)
- May not include all available resources
- May not reflect recent program changes

**A substitute for clinical judgment**
- Cannot replace your expertise and assessment
- Cannot account for patient-specific contraindications
- Cannot provide personalized care plans

### When NOT to Use NC-ASK

**Do not rely on NC-ASK for:**
- Emergency or crisis situations
- Time-sensitive clinical decisions
- Diagnostic determinations
- Treatment protocol selection
- Medication management
- Safety planning

**Use established clinical protocols and consultation for these scenarios.**

### AI-Specific Limitations

**Potential AI Issues:**

1. **Hallucination Risk**: AI may occasionally generate plausible-sounding but inaccurate information
   - **Mitigation**: Always verify through citations
   - Check source documents for critical information

2. **Context Limitations**: AI may not fully understand complex, nuanced queries
   - **Mitigation**: Simplify queries, ask follow-up questions
   - Break complex questions into parts

3. **Source Dependency**: Responses are only as good as source documents
   - **Mitigation**: Review citation quality
   - Supplement with your own knowledge and research

4. **No Clinical Reasoning**: AI synthesizes information but doesn't apply clinical judgment
   - **Mitigation**: Interpret responses through your clinical lens
   - Consider patient-specific factors AI cannot know

### Legal and Professional Disclaimer

**NC-ASK is an informational tool only.**

- Not approved as a medical device
- Not validated for clinical decision support
- Not a substitute for professional consultation
- Information provided "as is" without warranty

**Healthcare providers are solely responsible for:**
- Clinical assessments and diagnoses
- Treatment recommendations
- Patient safety
- Professional judgment and decision-making

**Document your independent clinical reasoning** when using NC-ASK information in patient care.

<a name="privacy-compliance"></a>
## Privacy and Compliance

### Patient Privacy Protection

**NC-ASK is designed with privacy by default:**

**No Personal Health Information (PHI) Required or Collected**
- No patient names
- No medical record numbers
- No dates of birth
- No specific diagnoses beyond what you choose to include in queries
- No treatment history

**Queries are anonymized:**
- System does not link queries to individual providers
- No login required (in most deployments)
- No tracking of user identity

### HIPAA Considerations

**NC-ASK and HIPAA:**

**Safe to use for general queries:**
- "What ABA providers serve Medicaid patients in Wake County?"
- "What are eligibility requirements for NC Innovations Waiver?"

**DO NOT include PHI in queries:**
- Do not include patient names
- Do not include medical record numbers
- Do not include specific identifying information
- Do not include detailed case histories

**Example - HIPAA Compliant Query:**
> "What transition planning resources are available for an 18-year-old with autism and moderate intellectual disability in Guilford County?"

**Example - Contains PHI (Do Not Use):**
> ~~"Where can I refer John Smith (DOB 1/15/2006, MRN 123456) who has autism and ID in Greensboro?"~~

### Data Logging

**What is logged (for system improvement and safety):**
- Queries asked (anonymized, no user identity)
- Crisis keyword detection events
- General usage statistics

**What is NOT logged:**
- User identity
- IP addresses (in privacy-focused deployments)
- Personal health information
- Patient identifiers

**Crisis Logging:**
If a query triggers crisis detection keywords, the system logs this for safety auditing purposes, but still without linking to user identity.

### Institutional Compliance

**For institutional deployments:**
- Consult your institution's IT and compliance teams
- Review your institution's AI usage policies
- Ensure NC-ASK deployment meets your security requirements
- Follow institutional protocols for clinical decision support tools

**Recommended:** Treat NC-ASK queries similar to searching public medical databases (PubMed, UpToDate) - use for information gathering, not for transmitting PHI.

<a name="crisis-detection-safety"></a>
# Crisis Detection and Safety (All Users)

## How Crisis Detection Works

NC-ASK includes built-in crisis detection to identify when a user may be experiencing a mental health emergency.

### Crisis Keywords

The system monitors for keywords indicating:
- **Suicide risk**: Suicidal thoughts, self-harm, ending life
- **Immediate danger**: Harm to self or others
- **Severe distress**: Acute mental health crisis

### What Happens When Crisis Keywords Are Detected

**Immediate Response:**
1. **Prominent crisis alert displayed** at the top of the page
2. **Crisis resources shown immediately** (988, Crisis Text Line, local resources)
3. **Standard response still provided** (but crisis resources take priority)

[Image: Crisis alert banner with resources prominently displayed]

**The system will:**
- Display 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline prominently
- Show Crisis Text Line information
- Provide NC Hope4NC Helpline
- Show 911 for immediate emergencies

**The system will NOT:**
- Call emergency services automatically
- Contact anyone on your behalf
- Share your information with crisis services (unless you contact them yourself)

### Crisis Resources

**For Immediate Danger:**
- **Call 911** for medical emergencies or immediate danger

**For Mental Health Crisis:**
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline**: Call or text 988 (24/7, free, confidential)
- **Crisis Text Line**: Text HELLO to 741741 (24/7, trained counselors)

**For North Carolina Support:**
- **NC Hope4NC Helpline**: 1-855-587-3463 (24/7 mental health support)

**For Autism-Specific Crisis Support:**
- Autism Society of North Carolina Crisis Line: [To be added]
- [Additional NC autism crisis resources to be added]

### Limitations of Crisis Detection

**Important:**
- Crisis detection is automated and may not catch all crisis situations
- If you are in crisis, please seek help immediately - do not rely on NC-ASK
- NC-ASK is not a crisis service and cannot provide emergency intervention

**If you or someone you know is in crisis:**
Call 988 or 911 immediately - do not wait for NC-ASK to detect crisis keywords.

<a name="faq"></a>
# Frequently Asked Questions

## For All Users

**Q: Do I need to create an account to use NC-ASK?**
A: No. NC-ASK is designed for anonymous use with no login required.

**Q: Is NC-ASK free?**
A: Yes. NC-ASK is a free resource provided to support the North Carolina autism community.

**Q: How current is the information?**
A: The knowledge base is updated periodically. Always check citation dates and verify time-sensitive information directly with organizations.

**Q: Can I use NC-ASK on my phone?**
A: Yes. NC-ASK is designed to work on desktop computers, tablets, and smartphones.

**Q: What if I can't find what I'm looking for?**
A: Try rephrasing your question with more specific details (location, age, service type). If you still can't find what you need, contact [support resource to be added] for assistance.

## For Patients and Caregivers

**Q: Is my information private?**
A: Yes. NC-ASK does not collect your name, email, or any personal identifying information. Your questions are anonymous.

**Q: Can NC-ASK diagnose autism?**
A: No. NC-ASK provides information about resources but cannot diagnose or assess individuals. Speak with a healthcare provider for diagnostic questions.

**Q: What if I need help right away?**
A: NC-ASK is not an emergency service. If you need immediate help, call 911 for emergencies or 988 for mental health crises.

**Q: Can NC-ASK help me in Spanish?**
A: Currently, NC-ASK is available in English only. Spanish language support is planned for future releases.

## For Healthcare Providers

**Q: Is NC-ASK HIPAA compliant?**
A: NC-ASK is designed with privacy by default and does not collect PHI. However, you should not include patient identifying information in your queries. Treat it as you would a public medical database search.

**Q: Can I use NC-ASK for clinical decision-making?**
A: NC-ASK provides information about resources and services but should not be used as the sole basis for clinical decisions. Apply your professional judgment and verify critical information.

**Q: How reliable are the sources?**
A: NC-ASK draws from authoritative sources (government agencies, medical institutions, established organizations). Always review citations and verify information for clinical use.

**Q: What if NC-ASK provides incorrect information?**
A: While NC-ASK strives for accuracy, AI systems can make errors. Always verify information through citations and contact organizations directly for critical decisions. Report errors to [contact to be added].
